Regulatory Environment

The Washington Medical Commission oversees physician licensing. Washington was an early adopter of full practice authority for nurse practitioners and has progressive telehealth laws. The state's Cascade Care program offers standardized health plans on the individual market.

Market Overview

Seattle is home to UW Medicine, Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center, and Virginia Mason Franciscan Health, creating a nationally competitive academic and specialty market. Amazon, Microsoft, and other tech companies' employee health demands have influenced provider competition in the Puget Sound region. Eastern Washington, centered on Spokane, is a distinct and more rural healthcare market.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many healthcare providers are in Washington?

Washington has over 68,000 active healthcare providers, including approximately 21,000 physicians, 4,800 dental practices, and 10,500 mental health professionals.

What makes Seattle a top healthcare market?

Seattle is home to UW Medicine, Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center (now Fred Hutch), and multiple major health systems. The city's tech economy drives demand for employer-sponsored healthcare and innovation in digital health.

How does eastern Washington differ from the Puget Sound for healthcare?

Eastern Washington, centered on Spokane, has significantly fewer providers per capita and a more rural demographic. Providence and MultiCare are the major systems there. Many residents in smaller communities travel to Spokane for specialty care.
