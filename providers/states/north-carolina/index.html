Regulatory Environment

The North Carolina Medical Board is one of the oldest state medical boards in the country. The state requires physician supervision for nurse practitioners and physician assistants. North Carolina recently expanded Medicaid, expected to cover hundreds of thousands of previously uninsured residents.

Market Overview

Duke Health, UNC Health, and Atrium Health (now Advocate Health) form a powerhouse triangle of academic and large-system medicine. The Research Triangle draws pharmaceutical and biotech investment that fuels clinical trials and provider demand. Charlotte is one of the fastest-growing healthcare markets in the Southeast.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many healthcare providers are in North Carolina?

North Carolina has over 95,000 active healthcare providers, including approximately 28,000 physicians, 5,800 dental practices, and 11,500 mental health professionals.

What makes the Research Triangle a healthcare hub?

The Raleigh-Durham area is home to Duke University Medical Center, UNC Hospitals, and a concentration of pharmaceutical and biotech companies. This creates a dense ecosystem of clinical and research-oriented healthcare providers.

Is healthcare growing in Charlotte?

Yes, Charlotte is one of the fastest-growing healthcare markets in the Southeast. Atrium Health (now part of Advocate Health) and Novant Health are the dominant systems, and the metro area continues to attract new providers and facilities.
