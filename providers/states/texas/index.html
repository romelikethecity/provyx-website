Regulatory Environment

The Texas Medical Board oversees one of the largest physician populations in the country. Texas doesn't have a state income tax, attracting physician relocations. The state has restrictive scope-of-practice laws for nurse practitioners, requiring physician supervision, and has not expanded Medicaid.

Market Overview

Houston's Texas Medical Center is the largest medical complex in the world, home to MD Anderson Cancer Center, Houston Methodist, and Texas Children's Hospital. Dallas-Fort Worth is one of the fastest-growing metro areas and healthcare markets nationally. Austin's tech-driven growth is rapidly expanding its healthcare needs. Texas's uninsured rate remains among the highest in the nation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many healthcare providers are in Texas?

Texas has over 250,000 active healthcare providers, including approximately 72,000 physicians, 16,500 dental practices, and 28,000 mental health professionals, making it the second-largest provider market in the U.S.

What makes the Texas Medical Center significant?

Houston's Texas Medical Center is the largest medical complex on earth, with over 60 institutions, 10 million patient encounters annually, and more than 100,000 employees. MD Anderson is the world's top-ranked cancer center.

What are the biggest healthcare challenges in Texas?

Texas has the highest uninsured rate of any large state, driven by its decision not to expand Medicaid. Rural West Texas and the Rio Grande Valley face severe provider shortages. Despite this, the state's metro areas continue to attract providers due to no state income tax and population growth.
