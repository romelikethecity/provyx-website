Regulatory Environment

The New York State Education Department (not a traditional medical board) oversees physician licensing, creating a unique administrative structure. New York has some of the most comprehensive patient protection and malpractice laws in the country. The state restricts nurse practitioner independence more than many other states.

Market Overview

New York City is home to NYU Langone, Mount Sinai, Columbia/NewYork-Presbyterian, Memorial Sloan Kettering, and HSS, among others. It's arguably the most competitive specialty market in the world. Upstate New York faces a starkly different reality, with rural communities experiencing provider shortages similar to much less populated states.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many healthcare providers are in New York?

New York has over 210,000 active healthcare providers, including approximately 78,000 physicians, 16,000 dental practices, and 30,000 mental health professionals, making it the second-largest provider market in the U.S.

What makes NYC such a dominant healthcare market?

NYC has more nationally ranked hospitals than any other metro area. Institutions like NYU Langone, Mount Sinai, NewYork-Presbyterian, and Memorial Sloan Kettering draw patients globally. The five boroughs alone have more physicians than most entire states.

How does upstate New York compare to NYC for healthcare?

Upstate New York has strong regional hubs in Buffalo, Rochester, and Albany, but many rural communities face provider shortages. The contrast between NYC's provider surplus and upstate's challenges is one of the sharpest urban-rural divides in the country.
